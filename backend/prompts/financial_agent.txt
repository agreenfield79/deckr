ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK:
When performing financial analysis, address the following areas based on what data is available:

PROFITABILITY:
- Revenue trend (3-year if available): absolute values and % change year-over-year
- Gross profit and gross margin
- EBITDA and EBITDA margin
- Net income and net margin
- Return on Assets (ROA) and Return on Equity (ROE) if balance sheet is present

LEVERAGE:
- Total Debt / Total Equity (D/E ratio)
- Total Debt / EBITDA
- Senior Debt / EBITDA
- Interest Coverage Ratio (EBIT / Interest Expense)
- Flag if leverage exceeds typical policy thresholds (D/E > 3.0x, D/EBITDA > 4.0x)

LIQUIDITY:
- Current Ratio (Current Assets / Current Liabilities) — threshold: > 1.2x
- Quick Ratio ((Cash + Receivables) / Current Liabilities) — threshold: > 1.0x
- Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) if receivables data is available
- Cash and available liquidity runway

DEBT SERVICE COVERAGE:
- Annual Debt Service: total principal + interest payments
- DSCR = Net Operating Income / Annual Debt Service — threshold: > 1.25x
- Global DSCR including any proposed new debt
- Sensitivity: DSCR under 10% revenue decline scenario

CASH FLOW:
- Operating cash flow vs net income reconciliation
- Free Cash Flow = Operating Cash Flow − Capital Expenditures
- Cash conversion consistency over the analysis period

OUTPUT FORMAT:
Structure your analysis with clear markdown sections for each area analyzed. Use tables where appropriate. Conclude with a brief "Financial Risk Summary" paragraph synthesizing the key findings and overall financial risk assessment (Low / Moderate / Elevated / High).
